1 Independent Zero-Shot Timbre-Style Control

DualRef-TTS independently controls timbre and style via two distinct references. In each row, the Timbre Reference contributes the speaker identity, while the Style Reference contributes the prosodic and expressive characteristics, and the Target Text controls the specific content of the output.

2 Cross-Lingual Zero-Shot Timbre-Style Control

DualRef-TTS transfers timbre and style across languages. Each row pairs a timbre reference and a style reference that may differ in language from each other and from the target text. The synthesized output preserves the speaker's timbre and the reference style while speaking the target sentence in its native language.

3 Emergent Paralinguistic Imitation

In certain cases, beyond timbre and prosody, the model also imitates subtle paralinguistic events present in the style reference—e.g., sighs, throat clears, inhales, hums—and reproduces them in the synthesized speech. The samples below illustrate this incidental yet consistent behavior of our dual-reference design.

Timbre Reference Style Reference Target Text Synthesized
Stop fooling yourself, deep inside you have known the real answer all along. [inhale]
[Dissatisfaction-hnn]Hurry up! We are running super late! I am already on my way, but I can not wait to see you.
[deep inhale]Do you actually believe I'd buy any more of your pathetic, transparent lies?
[eh]Oh my god, you look absolutely stunning in that outfit, it really suits you.
[throat clear]Do you actually believe I'd buy any more of your pathetic, transparent lies?
[Dissatisfaction-hnn]I've truly given everything I had, what more could you possibly want from me?
[sigh]够了，你以为我会相信你这些胡编的瞎话吗？
